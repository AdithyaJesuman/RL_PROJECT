1. The Problem (Stockouts)

In traditional inventory management, ordering is static. Here is what happens:

  • 📦 Baseline: We have 20 items in stock.
  • 🚚 Action: System blindly orders 15 items.
  • 🛒 Event: A massive weekend surge hits (Demand = 38).
  • 💥 Result: 20 + 15 = 35. We needed 38. STOCKOUT! We lose sales and customer trust.
🧠

2. How the RL Model Fixes It

We trained a Deep Q-Network (DQN) that tracks two state variables:

State = [Current Inventory %, Is_Weekend]
  • 👁️ Observation: The AI sees it's Thursday (tomorrow = weekend) and stock is 45.
  • ⚡ Action: Instead of a normal order, it takes action `30` to aggressively pre-stock.
  • 💰 Reward Logic: It maximizes ((Sales * Price) - Holding Cost - Stockout Penalty).
📊

3. Inside the Brain: SHAP Feature Importance

How does the AI know when to buy? We use SHAP to literally look inside its neural network and see which variables drive its decisions. Red dots mean high feature value.

What this graph tells us:

• Is Weekend (Top Row): You can see a massive cluster of red dots pushed far to the right. This proves the network learned that a weekend surge strongly INCREASES the urgency to place a massive order.

• Current Inventory (Bottom Row): Blue dots (low inventory) push the model to the right (order more). Red dots (high inventory) are strictly pushed left (order nothing), perfectly learning to minimize holding costs.
